Tribute to
"General Antonio Luna"

“He who submits to tyranny is more of a slave than the tyrant.” – Antonio Luna


Who Was General Antonio Luna?

General Antonio Luna (1866–1899) was one of the most brilliant military leaders during the Philippine-American War. Known for his fierce patriotism, sharp intellect, and unyielding discipline, Luna aimed to create a strong, united Filipino army. Though his temper was infamous, his loyalty to the Philippines was unquestionable.



The Legacy of Antonio Luna

  • Founded the short-lived but influential military academy of the Philippines.
  • Led key battles, including the defense at Calumpit and the battle in Pampanga.
  • Pushed for a united resistance against American occupation, despite internal conflicts among Filipino leaders.


Important Moments in His Life

  1. Graduated as a pharmacist and pursued advanced studies in Europe.
  2. Became editor of the newspaper *La Independencia*, using it to inspire nationalist sentiments.
  3. Assassinated in Cabanatuan in 1899, a tragic loss during the critical phase of the Philippine-American War.

A Message of Tribute

To the memory of General Antonio Luna, your dedication and sacrifice for the Philippines remain an enduring inspiration. You taught us the importance of discipline, unity, and bravery, even in the face of overwhelming odds. Your life was cut short, but your dream of an independent and sovereign Philippines lives on. May we honor your legacy by standing firm against all forms of oppression and treachery, continuing the fight for the freedom you gave your life for. Maraming salamat, Heneral Luna.
